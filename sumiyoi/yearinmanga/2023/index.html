we've shifted the focus to our lovely Idols who have gone beyond what was allowed to tell us the story about their hardships, their achievements, and their attempts at love. and it's not just the Idols that we usually see in concerts and television -- it can go as far as cabaret clubs, a train station, to even in the classroom!

the 25 mangas shown below have proved to break borders and warm our hearts with their presences.

I would like to personally applaud Yoshie for her support towards Hiyori despite the circumstances -- it makes me think that there's still much to be done to alleviate the suffering the Idols have inflicted.

Yo's profession as a florist has attracted a number of customers, that it had the attention of the customers from a specific cabaret club -- where Shinobu used to work. The past history both had shared (which was revealed in the later parts of the story) has rekindled their feelings, though I'm fairly certain that it's the memories along the way that made them bond together like a strong glue.

Overall, the experience was worth the wait as I've come across this many times; it's actually refreshing to see related stories that anticipates one's heart to be able to reap the fruit of love and labor.

In any case, Yo has melted Shinobu's icy heart and bloomed with hazel flowers on their souls.

AoHaru Manga Library made its mark in the video manga genre with repeated names such as Keiichi, Nagisa, Yui, and Noa. But regardless of the nameschemes, it's the stories that draws me in to the genre, and this is one of them!!

Keiichi Sato works at some sort of a cabaret club (that somehow has a similar vibe like the last one), but then gets ridiculed by no other than Narita. (That name sounds awful imo.)

Anyhow, what followed was a slew of circumstances and ways to work out sets of issues; as well as finding ways to challenge the norm. In the end, the three -- Noa, Yui, and Nagisa -- had their hearts inch closer to Keiichi's.

Quoting @RayKaz25: "Sometimes it takes a real man to become the best girl." This points to the fact that Keiichi had gone undercover (as Keina) to rule out Noa's biggest circumstance in the story. Narita got what he deserved in the end, which is a nice outcome I'd like to see from a typical antagonist.

Out-of-character, Keiichi being Keina is probably what I'll be feeling when I do feminine-related things. And since I'll be doing cosplays, I figured I'll take on that approach.
